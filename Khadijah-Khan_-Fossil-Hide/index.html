rethinking your eCommerce experience

Fossil & Hide is a local Atlanta jewelry business run by Karen Horn and Jenny Watts. As their presence increasing around Atlanta and beyond, their website needed an overhaul to increase direct sales and brand awareness. Our team chose to redesign the Fossil & Hide website to help this small business achieve their career goals and make a beautiful eCommerce site.

Problem Statement

Our research showed that Fossil & Hide’s website wasn’t maximizing its opportunity to provide an effortless shopping experience nor exhibit their bold style. During our research of shoppers’ jewelry and fashion preferences, we discovered just how significant an impact a piece of jewelry can have not only on one’s wardrobe but also on one’s state of mind and sense of self

UX Hypothesis

Therefore, we believe that to better sell the uniqueness and exclusivity of a jewelry statement piece we need to highlight each piece’s story, and that we might be able to help Fossil & Hide if we speak more directly to the thought, time, and effort they put into their work. We might do this by showcasing the jewelers performing their craft, offering more content for each item page, and limiting the amount of images per page so that the photographs become the minimalist statement pieces for the webpage just as their jewelry is meant to be for their customers. Doing this will allow our product to elevate the Fossil & Hide brand and allow for an increase of demand and reputation.

team

my role

timeline

We were given approximately 3 weeks to complete all stages of the design process. This timeline was compounded by all three teammates also having full-time jobs — -meaning every free moment counted! The scope of the project consisted of redesigning the essential elements of the website.

meet our stakeholders:

Our stakeholders, Jenny and Karen are bold local entrenprenuers in Georgia. Their desire to push the limits is exquistely apparent in the jewelry they create.

User Research: Our Target Users

The Fossil & Hide target users are women in the late-twenties to early forties who crave jewelry to serve as the statement piece that display their unique individualism. The F&H user understands that great jewelry is made with care and craftsmanship. We took on this mindset to produce our user personas. Strong women who are empowered by their fashion choices to be bold.

Affinity and Empathy Mapping

The process of affinity diagramming provided us a greater insight on what the majority of our users desired when shopping online for jewlery.

We managed to categorize our findings into 4 major groups:

  • Awareness
  • Consideration
  • Decision-making
  • post-purchase behavior

    • business canvas model

    The implementation of a business canvas model replenished a deeper understanding of the stakeholders of Fossil & Hide. As we approached the redesign of the eCommerce experience, it was essential to utilize the diffeent channels that currently brought consumers to the website. Social media and word of mouth played a major role of building the brand. Most of their purchases were completed through Instagram. We wanted to shift that form of purchase to their main website. It was crucial to pull our social media audience and to build a bridge between both platforms.

    User Interface

    UI Design Direction

    From the first glance, we felt the current webiste of Fossil & Hide lacked a vibrant presence. Jenny and Karen's mission was not visually communicated, and we felt the need to take a step forward and rethink their digital platform. The UX research provided a greater insight on our user's desires and needs. Now it was our turn to merge our users and stakeholders needs and wants.

    Style Guide

    As a designer, I was extremly excited to add the bold flare of Jenny and Karen's mission. We took the advantage of this opportunity and aimed to provide an appealing experience for our users. Every visual aspect of this website was developed in order to increase interactivity, which was not present in the original design. However, since this was our first iteration of the UI phase, we felt as a team thre is still room to improve our interface.

    UI Card Styles: The hover hint of purchase

    Exposing and highlighting the jewelry was an important aspect in all of our cards. Each card provided the user an opportunity to hover over the displayed jewelry pieces, and directed to the the page it was located on.
